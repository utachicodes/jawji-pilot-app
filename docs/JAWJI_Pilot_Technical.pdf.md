## Abstract

JAWJI Pilot is a revolutionary platform that connects people who need deliveries with certified drone operators anywhere in the world. Think Uber, but for drones—with one crucial difference: our operators can work from anywhere, creating new job opportunities globally while providing faster, safer, and more reliable delivery services.

Our app works seamlessly on both iOS and Android, making it accessible to everyone. The platform features intuitive flight controls that feel like a video game, real-time tracking, and smart safety features that keep drones working even when GPS signals are weak—like between tall buildings in cities.

What makes JAWJI special is our vision: we're not just building a delivery app. We're creating a platform that will expand to serve hospitals, restaurants, fire departments, mechanics, and more. Our modular plugin system allows drones to carry different tools depending on the mission—medical supplies, food, firefighting equipment, or repair tools.

This document explains how JAWJI works, why it's better than existing solutions, and our plans to become a key player across multiple industries.

## 2. How JAWJI Works: Simple for Users, Powerful Under the Hood

### For Clients (People Who Need Deliveries)

Using JAWJI is as simple as ordering a ride. Open the app, enter your pickup and drop-off locations, describe your package, and get an instant price quote. Once you confirm, you can watch your delivery in real-time on a map, see the drone's altitude and speed, and chat with the operator if needed.

### For Operators (Drone Pilots)

Becoming an operator is straightforward. After certification, operators simply open the app, go "online," and start receiving delivery requests. Each request shows the distance, estimated earnings, and package details. Operators can accept or decline—it's their choice.

During a mission, operators use intuitive virtual joysticks on their phone to control the drone. The app shows real-time telemetry (altitude, speed, battery level) and provides emergency controls like "Return to Home" or "Emergency Land."

### The Smart Technology Behind It

While the user experience is simple, the technology is sophisticated:

- **Low-latency control** - Operators feel like they're directly controlling the drone, with less than 100 milliseconds of delay
- **GPS-denied navigation** - When GPS signals are weak (like in urban canyons), our drones use computer vision to navigate safely
- **Obstacle avoidance** - Drones automatically detect and avoid obstacles, including people
- **Human recognition** - Drones can identify the recipient at the drop-off location (with consent) to ensure packages go to the right person

## 8. How the Plugin System Works

### For Operators

Operators can see which missions require which modules. A medical delivery might require a temperature-controlled module, while a firefighting mission needs a water dispenser. Operators choose missions based on their equipment and preferences.

### For Clients

Clients select the type of service they need. The app automatically matches them with operators who have the right equipment. A hospital ordering blood samples gets matched with operators who have medical modules.

This creates an ecosystem where innovation can come from anywhere.

## 9. Safety First: How We Keep Everyone Safe

### Multiple Layers of Protection

**Geofencing**: Drones automatically avoid restricted areas like airports, military bases, and national parks.

**Obstacle avoidance**: Computer vision detects and avoids obstacles in real-time, including buildings, trees, and people.

**Emergency controls**: Operators can instantly trigger "Return to Home" or "Emergency Land" if something goes wrong.

**Battery monitoring**: Drones automatically return home when battery levels get low.

**Weather awareness**: The system warns operators about high winds, rain, or other dangerous conditions.

### Operator Training

All operators must complete comprehensive training covering:

- Flight controls and emergency procedures
- Airspace regulations and geofencing
- Weather assessment
- Equipment maintenance
- Customer service

Operators are tested and certified before they can accept missions.

### Continuous Monitoring

Every mission is logged and monitored. If an operator has safety incidents, they're flagged for additional training or suspension. This ensures only qualified operators remain active on the platform.
